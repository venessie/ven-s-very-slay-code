Across the globe, every nation maintains a designated capital city, which serves as its primary seat of government and administrative hub. While many capitals, such as Paris, France, Tokyo, Japan, and Washington, D.C., USA, are also the largest or most prominent urban centers in their respective countries, this is not a universal rule.

For instance, the capital of Australia is Canberra, not the more populous Sydney or Melbourne.
